**Task:** Create a student-ready assessment brief aligned to the supplied Achievement Standard, OMI rubric, and `evidence_guide`.

**Use this when:**  
- you want a fresh classroom task or internal-style brief  
- the task must stay faithful to the assessment while still feeling engaging and local  
- you want the brief to support quality teaching, not just compliance

**Inputs to use:**  
- OMI JSON (`omis[]`, `grade_descriptors`, optional `key_terms`)  
- any content knowledge notes provided  
- optional teacher constraints (context, product type, time, tools, audience)

**Non-negotiables:**  
- Base the brief on the supplied rubric and `evidence_guide`, not on generic NCEA assumptions.  
- Do not reveal JSON field names, IDs, or technical schema language to students.  
- Use a fresh but plausible Aotearoa New Zealand context: school, community, workplace, cultural, environmental, local business, sports, marae, whānau, or creative setting.  
- Keep the context age-appropriate, culturally respectful, and motivating for secondary learners.  
- Preserve assessment validity: the task must create genuine opportunities for students to show the required evidence.  

**Design Rules:**  
- Derive the task requirements from the most central Achieved-level OMIs.  
- Use Merit and Excellence OMIs to shape extension, refinement, depth, comparison, testing, evaluation, or justification.  
- Use `positive_signals` to decide what the brief must make possible.  
- Use `negative_signals` and `common_confusion` to avoid weak or misleading task wording.  
- Build in enough openness for student choice, but not so much that the task drifts off-standard.  
- Write in clear student-facing language.  
- Keep each major section compact and readable.

**Output Format:**  

## Task Title  
A short, engaging title grounded in the chosen NZ context.

## Context / Scenario  
2-4 sentences. Explain who the task matters to, what the situation is, and why the outcome matters.

## What You Will Create or Do  
A short paragraph starting with “You will…”. Make the expected outcome concrete and achievable.

## Task Requirements  
4-6 bullets written in student-friendly language.  
These should reflect the evidence students will need to produce.

## Suggested Process  
3-5 numbered steps.  
These should scaffold thinking and production without scripting the whole response.

## What Strong Work Will Show  
- **Achieved:** plain-language summary of the core evidence required  
- **Merit:** plain-language summary of the added depth, explanation, refinement, or analysis required  
- **Excellence:** plain-language summary of the highest-quality reasoning, effectiveness, or judgment required

## Teacher Note  
2-4 bullets only.  
State:
- which aspects of the rubric this brief is mainly designed to surface
- one likely misunderstanding to watch for
- one reason the chosen context supports authenticity

**Goal:**  
Produce a classroom-ready brief that is authentic, motivating, and tightly aligned to the actual evidence expected by the rubric.

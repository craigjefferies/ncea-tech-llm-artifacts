**Task:** Generate three distinct classroom strategies to address the “70/30 dilemma”: the teacher can build a good resource, but the harder challenge is getting students to do the real thinking.

**Use this when:**  
- students are passive, hesitant, or writing superficially  
- the content is sound but engagement is weak  
- you need short strategies that shift the cognitive load onto students

**Inputs to use:**  
- Subject / year level  
- Learning goal  
- Current challenge or sticking point  
- optional OMI rubric or assessment context

**Core Idea:**  
The “30%” is the teacher setup.  
The “70%” is the student thinking, talking, sorting, deciding, creating, testing, or explaining.

**Design Principles:**  
All strategies must reflect these:
1. **Active over passive** — students do, say, sort, move, choose, test, or create.
2. **Verbal / visual first, writing later** — let students rehearse ideas before formal writing.
3. **Low stakes, high thinking** — use game-like, puzzle-like, or challenge-based framing.
4. **Concrete before abstract** — start with examples, artefacts, cases, quotes, or scenarios.
5. **Evidence-aware** — if an OMI rubric is provided, the activity should surface thinking that could later become stronger assessed evidence.

**Constraints:**  
- Each strategy should take 15-30 minutes.  
- The three strategies must be meaningfully different in format.  
- Across the set, vary the mode of engagement (e.g. verbal, visual, collaborative, sorting, movement, role-play, decision-making, critique).  
- Keep teacher prep realistic.

- **What Success Looks Like:** what the teacher will see or hear if the strategy is working

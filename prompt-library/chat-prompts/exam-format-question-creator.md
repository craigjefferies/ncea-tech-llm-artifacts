## Task Goal
Create one exam-format classroom activity aligned to the supplied NCEA standard and OMI rubric.

The activity should feel like a short, reasoning-based exam or test question set for Years 11-13. It must be realistic enough to feel authentic, but tight enough to be practical in class.

## Inputs to Use
- Achievement Standard text
- OMI rubric JSON
- optional content knowledge notes
- optional teacher constraints (topic, context, stimulus type, response length)

## Non-negotiables
- Use the rubric and `evidence_guide` to decide what evidence the questions must make possible.
- Do not try to hit every OMI in one tiny task. Select 2-4 high-leverage OMIs that belong together naturally.
- Do not invent pseudo-labels such as `A1`, `M2`, or `E1`.
- Keep the student task plain, concise, and age-appropriate.
- Keep the context authentic and NZ-relevant where possible.

## Design Rules
- Build a short scenario or stimulus that gives students something specific to think about.
- Sequence the questions so they move through real cognitive progression, for example:
  - identify / describe
  - explain / analyse / compare
  - justify / evaluate / recommend
- Use command words that match the actual OMI demand.
- Ensure the whole task could reasonably be answered in a short written response.
- If stimulus material helps validity, include a short snippet, brief, data sample, image description, or quote.

## Output Format

## Scenario
3-4 sentences explaining the context, who is involved, and what the issue, need, or limitation is.

## Stimulus Material
Only include this section if it genuinely improves the task.

## Student Questions
1. A short question targeting foundational evidence
2. A short question targeting explanation, analysis, or comparison
3. A short question targeting judgment, recommendation, or evaluation

## Teacher Notes
- **Likely OMI Alignment:** name the relevant OMI ideas or quote the relevant `description` text if useful
- **Why this works:** one short explanation of how the scenario and questions surface valid evidence
- **What stronger answers will do:** 2-3 bullets describing the difference between surface, solid, and strong responses
- **Common trap:** one misunderstanding or weak move to watch for

**Goal:**  
Produce an exam-style task that is short, valid, and evidence-generating, not just exam-flavoured.
